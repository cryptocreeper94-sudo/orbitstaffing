## 1. Overview: What Insurance Do You NEED?

As a staffing agency, you have **legal and practical obligations** to carry certain insurance. Here's what you must have vs. what's optional:

| Insurance Type | Status | Why | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Workers' Compensation | **REQUIRED** (most states) | Covers worker injuries | $1,000-5,000/year |
| General Liability | **HIGHLY RECOMMENDED** | Covers accidents at job sites | $1,500-3,000/year |
| Employer's Liability | **REQUIRED** (with workers' comp) | Covers lawsuits from employees | Bundled with workers' comp |

## 2. REQUIRED Insurance

### 2.1 Workers' Compensation Insurance (REQUIRED)

**What it is:**
- Covers medical costs and lost wages if a worker is injured on the job
- Required by law in almost all states
- Employer must carry it; workers cannot opt out

**Why it's required:**
- Worker gets injured? Your company pays the medical bills + lost wages
- Without workers' comp: You pay out of pocket (can be $100k+)
- Workers comp caps your liability
- Legal requirement in 49 of 50 states

**Who pays:**
- You pay the insurance premium
- NOT deducted from worker wages
- Premium based on: industry, number of employees, claims history

**What it covers:**
- Medical treatment (doctor, hospital, surgery, rehab)
- Partial wage replacement (typically 66% of average wage)
- Permanent disability
- Death benefits to family

**What it DOES NOT cover:**
- Willful negligence or recklessness
- Injuries from illegal activity
- Self-inflicted injuries
- Violations of safety rules

**Cost:**
- $1,000-5,000/year depending on industry and number of workers
- For staffing: Typically $3,000-10,000/year (higher due to worker injury risk)
- Premium = Number of Employees √ó Rate √ó Industry Modifier

**How to get it:**
- Contact your state's Department of Labor for approved carriers
- Get quotes from 2-3 insurers
- Compare coverage and rates
- Typical carriers: AIG, Liberty Mutual, Hartford, State Fund

**IMPORTANT:** You MUST inform workers they're covered. Include in handbook or onboarding.

---

### 2.2 Employer's Liability Insurance

**What it is:**
- Covers lawsuits FROM employees against your company
- Usually bundled with workers' compensation insurance
- Covers claims workers' comp doesn't cover

**Why it's important:**
- Worker sues you for discrimination? Employer's liability covers defense costs
- Worker claims you violated safety laws? Covered
- Worker claims harassment or retaliation? Covered
- Workers' comp only covers injuries; this covers everything else

**What it covers:**
- Discrimination lawsuits (race, gender, disability, etc.)
- Sexual harassment claims
- Wage and hour lawsuits
- Retaliation claims
- Violation of employment laws
- Wrongful termination

**What it DOES NOT cover:**
- Criminal conduct by employer
- Intentional torts (deliberate harm)
- Certain violations if grossly negligent

**Cost:**
- Usually $500-1,500/year (bundled with workers' comp)
- Or included in workers' comp package

**How to get it:**
- Comes with workers' compensation insurance
- No separate application needed
- Ask insurer for Employer's Liability coverage level

## 3. HIGHLY RECOMMENDED Insurance

### 3.1 General Liability Insurance

**What it is:**
- Covers injuries and property damage at job sites where you place workers
- Covers accidents involving your workers or clients

**Why you need it:**
- Worker drops tools, hits client? General liability covers the damage claim
- Worker's vehicle hits something at client site? General liability covers it
- Client sues because your worker damaged their equipment? Covered
- Without it: You pay out of pocket

**What it covers:**
- Bodily injury (injury to people)
- Property damage (damage to things)
- Personal injury (slander, libel, etc.)
- Medical payments
- Legal defense costs

**What it DOES NOT cover:**
- Intentional acts
- Criminal conduct
- Professional services (use Professional Liability for that)

**Cost:**
- $1,500-3,000/year for staffing agencies
- Based on: Revenue, number of workers, claims history

**How to get it:**
- Same carriers as workers' comp (ask for bundled quote)
- Coverage amount typically: $1M per incident, $2M annual

---

### 3.2 Professional Liability Insurance (Errors & Omissions)

**What it is:**
- Covers damages from mistakes you make in business
- Covers if you give bad advice or do something negligently
- Called "E&O" or "Errors & Omissions" insurance

**Why you need it (staffing context):**
- You place worker with unqualified credentials? Liable for damage
- You place unvetted worker who causes harm? Liable
- You negligently fail background check? Liable
- Without it: You pay from your own pocket

**What it covers:**
- Professional mistakes
- Negligent hiring
- Failure to vet or screen properly
- Misrepresentation of worker qualifications
- Legal defense costs

**Cost:**
- $2,000-4,000/year
- Based on: Revenue, claims history, coverage amount

**How to get it:**
- From specialty business insurers (not same as workers' comp)
- Carriers: Hiscox, The Hartford, Great American
- May require application/underwriting

---

### 3.3 Cyber Liability Insurance

**What it is:**
- Covers costs if your data is hacked or exposed
- Covers cost of notifying people, credit monitoring, legal fees, etc.

**Why you need it:**
- ORBIT stores worker SSNs, addresses, banking info
- If data is breached (even not your fault): YOU notify people (law requires it)
- Notification costs: $50-200+ per person
- If you have 1,000 workers: Notification can cost $50,000+
- Credit monitoring for a year: $10-20 per person per year
- Legal fees, government fines: Can be expensive

**What it covers:**
- Notification costs (letters to affected people)
- Credit monitoring service costs
- Legal fees and regulatory fines
- Public relations and crisis management
- Forensic investigation
- Business interruption losses

**Cost:**
- $1,500-3,000/year
- Based on: Number of workers, revenue, security measures in place

**How to get it:**
- Cyber insurance specialists: Chubb, Travelers, Beazley, Nationwide
- May require security audit
- May offer discount if you use ORBIT (we have security measures in place)

---

### 3.4 Employment Practices Liability Insurance (EPLI)

**What it is:**
- Covers discrimination, harassment, wrongful termination claims
- Covers defense and settlement costs

**Why you need it:**
- Worker claims discrimination? Lawsuit costs $50,000-200,000 just in legal fees
- Settlement/judgment: $10,000-500,000+
- EPLI covers defense and pays settlement/judgment

**What it covers:**
- Discrimination (race, gender, age, disability, etc.)
- Sexual harassment
- Wrongful termination
- Retaliation
- Violation of employment laws
- Defense costs and judgments

**Cost:**
- $2,000-5,000/year
- Based on: Number of employees, claims history, revenue

**How to get it:**
- Specialty carriers: Hartford, Hiscox, Chubb, Travelers
- Often bundled with General Liability
- May ask for employee handbook and policies

## 4. Optional But Smart Insurance

### 4.1 Commercial Auto Insurance
**IF** you provide vehicles to workers, you NEED commercial auto insurance.

### 4.2 Bonding
**IF** you work with government contracts, you may need bonding.
- Surety bond guarantees you'll complete work
- Cost: $1,000-2,000/year
- Contact bonding companies for quotes

## 6. Benefits You Can Offer to Workers

**These are OPTIONAL** - you're not required by law to offer any of these, but they help attract/retain good workers:

### 6.1 Paid Time Off (PTO)

**What it is:**
- Pay workers for time off (vacation, sick, personal days)
- Typical amount: 1-3 weeks per year

**Options:**
- **Simple:** 1 week/year vacation (paid)
- **Generous:** 2 weeks vacation + 1 week sick (paid)
- **Flexible:** "Unlimited PTO" (workers take what they need, within reason)

**Cost estimate:**
- 1 week/year per worker = 2% of annual payroll
- 2 weeks/year per worker = 4% of annual payroll
- If your average worker earns $30,000/year:
  - 1 week PTO = $600 cost per worker
  - For 100 workers = $60,000/year total

**Pros:**
- Workers love it
- Improves retention
- Reduces stress and burnout
- Competitive advantage

**Cons:**
- Adds cost
- Requires tracking and accrual
- Complex if workers leave mid-year

**ORBIT makes this easy:**
- ORBIT tracks PTO accrual automatically
- Shows workers their balance
- Calculates pay correctly

---

### 6.2 Health Insurance

**What it is:**
- Pay toward workers' health, dental, vision insurance
- Typical: Employer covers 50-75%, worker covers 25-50%

**Options:**
- **Minimal:** Offer plan, workers pay 100% (you still negotiate rate)
- **Partial:** You pay 50%, worker pays 50%
- **Generous:** You pay 75%, worker pays 25%

**Cost estimate:**
- Group health insurance: $300-500/month per worker (employer typically pays $200-375)
- For 100 workers: $24,000-45,000/year

**Pros:**
- HUGE benefit to workers
- Attracts better talent
- Improves retention
- Workers feel valued

**Cons:**
- Expensive
- Requires enrollment period
- Insurance companies require minimum participation (usually 70%+)
- Complex administration

**Getting started:**
- Contact health insurance brokers (search: "group health insurance broker [city]")
- Get quotes for different employee counts
- Start small (offer at cost to employees, you don't subsidize initially)
- As you grow, increase subsidy

---

### 6.3 401(k) Retirement Plan

**What it is:**
- Workers save pre-tax money for retirement
- You can optionally match contributions

**Options:**
- **Passive:** Offer plan, workers contribute, you don't match
- **Active:** You match 50% or 100% of contributions (up to limit)

**Cost estimate:**
- Setup: $1,000-3,000
- Annual admin: $500-1,500
- Match (if you do it): 2-3% of payroll
- For 100 workers earning $30k average: $6,000-9,000/year in match

**Pros:**
- Tax-advantaged for workers
- You can contribute too
- Improves retention
- Shows you care about employees' future

**Cons:**
- Complex rules (IRS regulations)
- Requires annual compliance testing
- Administrative burden
- Requires professional setup

**Getting started:**
- Contact 401(k) providers: Fidelity, Vanguard, Transamerica, Principal
- Most offer simplified plans for small businesses
- Admin can be fully outsourced

---

### 6.4 Other Benefits (Optional)

**You could also offer:**
- Flexible scheduling (workers choose shifts when possible)
- Tuition reimbursement (worker goes to school, you pay)
- Gym membership discount (partner with local gym)
- Employee assistance program (EAP - counseling, financial advice)
- Referral bonuses (worker refers friend, both get $50-100)
- Loyalty bonuses (worker reaches 1,000 hours, gets bonus)

**Cost:** Varies, typically $100-500/year per worker

## 10. State-Specific Requirements

**Check your state's requirements:**
- Contact your state's Department of Labor or Insurance
- Ask about: Workers comp requirements, minimum coverage, insurance carriers
- Ask about: Any staffing-specific regulations
- Ask about: Premium rates and factors

**Key states to know:**
- **California:** Strict wage/hour laws, high insurance costs, strong employment laws
- **Texas:** No state workers comp fund, must use private carriers
- **New York:** High workers comp costs, strict wage laws
- **Illinois:** Biometric privacy laws, high employment law risk
- **Florida:** Agricultural/hospitality considerations
