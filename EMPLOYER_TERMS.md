## 4. GPS Verification Terms

### 4.1 You Understand GPS
- GPS is accurate to within ¬±15-30 feet (95% of the time)
- GPS captures location only at clock-in/out (not continuous tracking)
- GPS data can be disputed and overridden by manual verification
- See "GPS Verification Policy" for complete details

### 4.2 You Agree To:
- Inform workers about GPS verification before first shift
- Provide them a copy of "GPS Verification Policy"
- Answer their questions about GPS
- Explain that GPS is for accuracy, not surveillance
- Clarify that location is NOT tracked between clock-in/out

### 4.3 You Agree NOT To:
- Track worker location between clock-in/out (that's surveillance)
- Use GPS data for purposes other than time/attendance
- Share GPS data with third parties without worker consent
- Discriminate against workers based on GPS data
- Use GPS data to monitor worker productivity/activity

### 4.4 Fair Dispute Resolution
- If a worker disputes GPS data, you must investigate
- Consider reasonable explanations (GPS failure, device issue)
- Don't automatically reject hours based on GPS alone
- Document all disputes and your resolution
- If unable to resolve, escalate to ORBIT arbitration
