## 2. Attendance & Scheduling Rules

### 2.1 Acceptance of Shifts
- You are NOT required to accept every shift offered
- When you accept a shift, you COMMIT to showing up
- Accept shifts only if you WILL show up
- Do not accept shifts you plan to cancel

### 2.2 On-Time Arrival
**Workers must arrive 10 minutes BEFORE scheduled start time.**

- Clock-in opens 10 minutes before start time
- You must be physically present to clock in
- GPS must verify you're at the location
- Clocking in late results in a verbal warning

**Definition of "Late":**
- If start time is 8:00 AM, you're late if you clock in after 8:00 AM
- A few minutes isn't the end of the world, but 15+ minutes has consequences

### 2.3 Tardiness Policy

**1st Tardy (15+ minutes late):**
- Verbal warning from employer
- Documented in your record
- Employer may reduce your hours that day
- You still get paid for hours worked

**2nd Tardy (within 30 days):**
- Written warning
- You may be removed from the shift
- You may be removed from similar shifts
- Documented in your employment record

**3rd Tardy (within 30 days):**
- Suspension from assignments
- Written notice of suspension
- Can reapply after 5 business days
- Next tardy results in termination

**4th Tardy or Pattern:**
- Immediate termination
- Removed from ORBIT platform
- Cannot reapply for 6 months

**Exception - Emergency:**
If you have a legitimate emergency (accident, illness), call your employer IMMEDIATELY:
- Explain the situation
- Provide estimated arrival time
- Employer may waive tardy with documentation
- Medical emergency = automatic exception with doctor's note

### 2.4 No-Shows (Not Showing Up)

**No-Show = You accepted a shift and didn't show up without telling employer.**

**1st No-Show:**
- Immediate removal from all active shifts
- Written warning
- Can reapply for shifts after 24 hours
- May need to explain why you didn't show

**2nd No-Show (within 30 days):**
- Suspended from platform for 7 days
- Must contact employer before reapplication
- Final written warning
- Any further no-shows = termination

**3rd No-Show:**
- Permanent removal from ORBIT platform
- Cannot reapply for 12 months
- Employer has right to bill you for any damage caused

**Exception - Emergency:**
Call/text employer ASAP with explanation:
- Medical emergency (require doctor's note)
- Family emergency (require documentation)
- Accident (require police report)
- Legitimate excuse with documentation = may avoid penalty

**Rule: Always communicate.** Even if you can't show up, a quick text prevents termination.

### 2.5 Calling Out of Shifts

**You can cancel a shift, but there are rules:**

**Cancellation 24+ hours before shift:**
- No penalty
- No documentation required
- Easy button in app: "Cancel Shift"
- Employer finds someone else
- Everyone's happy

**Cancellation 24-2 hours before shift:**
- Logged in system
- May result in a written note
- Employer may reduce your future shifts
- Repeated cancellations may affect job priority
- But you CAN cancel

**Cancellation less than 2 hours before:**
- Counts as a "call-out"
- Documented as professional violation
- After 2 call-outs in 30 days: warning
- After 3 call-outs in 30 days: suspension
- After 4+ in 60 days: termination

**Emergency Call-Outs:**
- Medical emergency: Just call. No penalty. Get doctor's note later.
- Family emergency: Call immediately. Explain. Documented but no penalty if legitimate.
- Accident: Call right away. No questions.

**DO NOT just not show up. ALWAYS COMMUNICATE.**

## 3. Conduct & Professional Standards

### 3.1 Respect & Professionalism
- Treat coworkers, employers, and clients with respect
- No harassment, bullying, or discrimination
- No threats, violence, or intimidation
- Be professional and courteous
- Represent ORBIT with integrity

### 3.2 Safety
- Follow all safety rules and OSHA guidelines
- Wear required PPE (hard hats, gloves, etc.)
- Report safety violations immediately
- Never work impaired (drugs, alcohol, or extreme fatigue)
- Report accidents to your employer right away
- Do not endanger yourself or others

### 3.3 Honesty & Integrity
- GPS verification must be honest
- Clock in/out at the actual job location
- Don't ask someone to clock in for you
- Don't falsify timesheet or work records
- Don't steal from employer or clients
- Don't damage property intentionally

### 3.4 Cell Phones & Distractions
- Keep phone secure during work
- Use phone for work communication only (unless breaks allowed)
- Don't use phone in safety-sensitive areas
- Don't operate machinery while using phone
- No excessive personal calls/texts during work
- Breaks: Use your phone freely on breaks

### 3.5 Appearance & Dress Code
- Follow employer's dress code
- Clean, professional appearance
- Required PPE must be worn
- If employer specifies uniform: wear it
- If employer specifies color: match it
- Tattoos/piercings: Follow employer rules

## 4. Breaks & Lunch Periods

### 4.1 Lunch Breaks
- Shifts 6+ hours: You're entitled to a lunch break
- Typically 30 minutes unpaid
- Must clock out when starting lunch
- Must clock in when returning from lunch
- Do NOT work through lunch
- ORBIT will calculate lunch break, you don't need to do anything

### 4.2 Rest Breaks
- Shifts 4+ hours: You may take a 10-15 minute rest break
- Paid time (you're still on the clock)
- Coordinate with supervisor for timing
- Don't abandon your post
- Clock does NOT stop for rest breaks (they're paid)

### 4.3 Break Rules
- Breaks are time to rest, not to do other work
- Can check phone, get water, sit down
- Don't use break time to do side jobs or errands
- Must be available to return if needed urgently
- Excessive break time may be disciplined

## 5. Wages & Payments

### 5.1 Pay Rate
- You're paid the rate posted on the job
- Rate set by employer, approved by client
- You know the rate before accepting the shift
- Rate cannot change mid-shift without your consent
- Rate must comply with minimum wage laws

### 5.2 Hours Worked
- Paid for all hours actually worked
- Clock-in and clock-out times determine pay
- Breaks: Unpaid (unless employer decides to pay)
- Lunch: Unpaid (unless employer decides to pay)
- GPS verifies your location at clock-in/out

### 5.3 Payment Schedule
- Workers paid weekly or bi-weekly (employer determines)
- Payment via direct deposit (no cash)
- Payday is consistent every week/every other week
- If payday is a holiday, you're paid the day before
- ORBIT processes payments automatically

### 5.4 Wage Disputes
- If you believe you're paid incorrectly:
  1. Contact your employer within 7 days
  2. Provide details (date, shift, hours, expected pay)
  3. Employer reviews and responds within 10 days
  4. If still disputed, escalate to ORBIT
  5. ORBIT arbitration if needed
- All disputes documented

### 5.5 Deductions
- Only legal deductions from pay:
  - Federal income tax
  - Social Security/Medicare (FICA)
  - State income tax
  - Court-ordered child support or wage garnishment
- NO deductions for:
  - Tools or equipment (employer must provide)
  - Uniforms (employer must provide)
  - Mistakes or damages (unless willful)
  - "Breakage" (not allowed)
- Ask if any deductions seem wrong

## 6. Time Off & Paid Time Off (PTO)

### 6.1 Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

**ORBIT Staffing OS does NOT automatically provide paid vacation, sick days, or personal days.**

However, employers using ORBIT may offer PTO. Check with your employer:
- Do they offer paid time off?
- How many days per year?
- Can you carry unused days to next year?
- Do you get paid out if you leave?

### 6.2 If Your Employer Offers PTO:

**Accrual:**
- Typical: 1-2 weeks per year (10-20 days)
- Accrues per paycheck (grows each week)
- You can see your PTO balance in the app

**Usage:**
- Request PTO in advance when possible
- Provide at least 2 weeks notice for vacation
- Can use for vacation, illness, or personal needs
- Employer approves or denies requests
- Denied requests: Discuss why with employer

**Sick Time (if offered):**
- Used when you're ill or injured
- Usually 3-5 days per year
- Some employers don't distinguish (just "PTO")
- Don't use sick time for non-illness reasons

**Personal Days (if offered):**
- Time off for personal reasons
- Usually 1-2 days per year
- Advance notice preferred but not always required
- Use responsibly

### 6.3 If Your Employer Does NOT Offer PTO:
- You can still request unpaid time off
- Employer may or may not approve
- If approved: You don't get paid for that time
- If denied: You must work or no-show (which is a violation)

### 6.4 Holidays
- ORBIT does not mandate paid holidays
- Some employers pay for holidays (New Year's, Christmas, etc.)
- Ask your employer which holidays are paid
- If not paid: You don't get paid if you don't work
- If paid: You get paid whether you work or not

## 7. Discrimination, Harassment & Bullying

### 7.1 Zero Tolerance Policy
ORBIT has a **zero tolerance** for discrimination and harassment.

**You cannot be treated unfairly because of:**
- Race
- Gender or gender identity
- Religion
- Age
- Disability
- National origin
- Sexual orientation
- Marital/family status
- Military status
- Any other protected status

### 7.2 Sexual Harassment
- Unwelcome sexual comments, advances, or requests
- Creating a hostile or intimidating environment
- Quid pro quo ("do this or lose your job")
- Is NOT tolerated
- Report to employer or ORBIT immediately

### 7.3 Bullying & Hostile Work Environment
- Threatening, intimidating, or offensive behavior
- Humiliation or belittling
- Exclusion or ostracism
- Repeated disrespect
- Will result in discipline or termination

### 7.4 What To Do If You Experience Discrimination:
1. Tell the person it's not okay (if safe to do)
2. Document what happened (date, time, who, what was said)
3. Report to your employer's manager
4. If no resolution: Report to ORBIT
5. ORBIT will investigate and take action
6. Your report is confidential

### 7.5 Retaliation
- You will NOT be punished for reporting discrimination
- Retaliation is ILLEGAL and will result in termination
- If you're retaliated against, report it immediately
- ORBIT protects whistleblowers

## 13. Progressive Discipline

Most violations follow this process:

**Step 1: Verbal Warning**
- Manager explains the issue
- You understand the expectation
- Documented in your file
- No pay impact

**Step 2: Written Warning**
- Formal documentation
- Signed by you and employer
- Notice of next step
- No pay impact (usually)

**Step 3: Suspension**
- Removed from shifts for 3-7 days
- Time to reflect
- May be unpaid
- Final chance to change behavior

**Step 4: Termination**
- You're removed from ORBIT
- May apply again after 30-90 days
- Repeated terminations = permanent ban

**Exceptions (Immediate Termination):**
- Theft or dishonesty
- Violence or threats
- Sexual harassment
- Discrimination
- Gross safety violation
- Working impaired
- GPS fraud
